# Lightning for academia

Lightning works for students through universities at scale. This page covers the common academic setups in one place. (Consolidates 9 previously separate pages.)

## Students

Lightning is built for homework, group projects, hackathons, and personal exploration. Students get the free tier (80 GPU hours/month with a `.edu` email) and a persistent Studio that survives between sessions.

> Common patterns: ML coursework, paper reproductions, capstone projects, hackathon prototypes.

## University classrooms

Instructors can publish a Studio as a class template. Every student clones it and gets the same environment, code, and dependencies. No "it works on my laptop" debugging.

> Common patterns: ML/AI courses, deep learning workshops, distributed systems labs.

## Research labs

Research labs use Lightning to manage shared compute, reproducible experiments, and collaborative environments. Studios are the unit of reproducibility: clone the Studio, get the exact environment a paper used.

> Common patterns: PhD experiments, multi-author projects, lab-wide GPU sharing.

## Research projects (multi-institution)

For projects spanning multiple universities, Teamspaces let you scope access per institution, share compute budgets, and consolidate billing.

> Common patterns: grant-funded collaborations, multi-PI projects, conference companion repos.

## Academic conferences

Conferences can use Studios to publish fully reproducible companion artifacts. Reviewers click a link, get the exact paper environment, and re-run the experiments. No Docker debugging.

> Common patterns: NeurIPS / ICLR / CVPR reproducibility tracks, workshop tutorials.

## Universities (institution-wide)

Lightning scales to institution-wide use. Centralized billing, IT-friendly admin tools, SSO, and audit logs let universities provide AI compute as a managed resource for students and faculty.

> Common patterns: campus-wide AI infrastructure, shared GPU pools, departmental teamspaces.
